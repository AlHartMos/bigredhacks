How Vigenère Ciphers Work

A Vigenère cipher is a way to hide a message by changing the letters so only someone with a secret key can read it.

The Idea

It works kind of like a secret code wheel. You use a word, like KEY, to shift the letters of your message.

Example

Let’s say you want to send this message:

HELLO

And your secret key is:

KEY

We repeat the key to match the length of the message:

KEYKE

Now we shift each letter in HELLO by the matching letter in the key:

  • H + K = R
  • E + E = I
  • L + Y = J
  • L + K = V
  • O + E = S

So the encrypted message is:

RIJVS

Why It’s Cool

Unlike simple ciphers (like Caesar), Vigenère ciphers use different shifts for each letter, making them much harder to crack without the key!

Fun Fact

People used Vigenère ciphers for hundreds of years to keep their messages secret. They were considered "unbreakable" for a long time!
